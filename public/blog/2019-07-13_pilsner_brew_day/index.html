Today is brew day, and I will be cooking the Brewer’s Pantry ‘Pilsners’ kit. This is my first attempt at an all-grain brew Brewing using grains as the main ingredient, rather than extract. , and the only additional equipment that I had to purchase was the mash tun Large insulated container used for steeping grains in hot water (see mash). and some 1⁄2 inch tubing! The jump from extract brewing Brewing using malt extract syrup to all-grain Brewing using grains as the main ingredient, rather than extract. was not as expensive as I thought, and the recipe kits for all-grain brew Brewing using grains as the main ingredient, rather than extract. are actually cheaper than the extract kits. The wonderful benefit to purchasing from the Brewer’s Pantry, is that they assemble the ingredients at time of order, at which point they will also mill the grain if you wish, at no additional cost. I’m still in shock that I discovered this small business in my old home town that will mill grain, at no additional cost, right when I purchase a kit! The alternative for me, since I am not interested in purchasing a milling machine, is to purchase a recipe kit that has had the grain milled sometime in the past and sitting on a shelf for who knows how long. With all that being said, I would like to mention that I am not being incentivised in any way by Brewer’s Pantry, and am just sharing my very positive experience. Now onto the brew!

Equipment

  • Mash Tun
  • 1⁄2 inch clear tubing
  • Brew kettle (I use an 8 gal kettle with thermometer and ball valve)
  • Giant soup pot (to heat sparge water)
  • Hose that fits into the barbed fitting that came with the mash tun
  • Giant spoon
  • Thief
  • Hydrometer
  • Fermentor
  • Teflon tape (for FastFerment, may not be needed depending on your fermentor)
  • Wort Chiller
  • Airlock

Cleaning Products

  • PBW
  • StarSan

Consumable Products

  • Brewer’s Pantry ‘Pilsners’ Kit (contains grain, hops, whirlfloc tab, and yeast)
  • Spring water (my city water is too hard so I buy the cheapest spring water I can)
