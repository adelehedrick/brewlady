Today is brew day, and I will be cooking the [Brewer's Pantry 'Pilsners' kit](http://brewerspantry.com/brewers-pantry-recipe-kits/all-grain-kits?product_id=821). This is my first attempt at an {{< tooltip "all-grain brew">}}{{< glossary "all-grain brewing">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, and the only additional equipment that I had to purchase was the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and some 1/2 inch tubing! The jump from {{< tooltip "extract brewing">}}{{< glossary "extract brewing">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to {{< tooltip "all-grain">}}{{< glossary "all-grain brewing">}}{{< /tooltip >}} was not as expensive as I thought, and the recipe kits for {{< tooltip "all-grain brew">}}{{< glossary "all-grain brewing">}}{{< /tooltip >}} are actually cheaper than the extract kits. The wonderful benefit to purchasing from the [Brewer’s Pantry](http://brewerspantry.com), is that they assemble the ingredients at time of order, at which point they will also mill the grain if you wish, at no additional cost. I’m still in shock that I discovered this small business in my old home town that will mill grain, at no additional cost, right when I purchase a kit! The alternative for me, since I am not interested in purchasing a milling machine, is to purchase a recipe kit that has had the grain milled sometime in the past and sitting on a shelf for who knows how long. With all that being said, I would like to mention that I am not being incentivised in any way by Brewer’s Pantry, and am just sharing my very positive experience. Now onto the brew!

# Brew Day Journal
First day with the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, so I cleaned it out with some PBW and rinsed well. If you are using any new equipment, or the equipment is noticeably dirty, then you should soak it overnight with PBW. Since my gear has been washed with PBW, drip dried and then stored in such a way as to avoid collecting dust, I’m happy to go straight to brewing with most of it.

{{< foldergallery src="img/blog/pilsner_brew_day_july2019/lauter_tun" >}}

The soup pot I intend to use to heat the {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}} was pretty darn dirty, so I used some PBW on that too.

Heated the water to be used in the initial {{< tooltip "mash">}}{{< glossary "mash">}}{{< /tooltip >}} in the {{< tooltip "brew kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}}. Used 4.5 gallons (used 2 quart to 1 lb grain which converts to 4.25 gallons of water which I rounded up to 4.5 gallons). {{< tooltip "Strike water">}}{{< glossary "strike water temperature">}}{{< /tooltip >}} calculation results in 157.8&deg;F.

{{< foldergallery src="img/blog/pilsner_brew_day_july2019/heating_water" >}}

Used heated water to rinse the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and return back to the {{< tooltip "brew kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to heat the tun a bit before steeping. 

Added grains to the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, and by the time I checked the {{< tooltip "brew kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, the temp went past 160&deg;F. So much for pre-heating the tun!


Next time I’m going to run my tea kettle and add some hot water to the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to just heat it, and then purge it when it’s time to add grains and water.

In this case I’m going to use the {{< tooltip "strike temperature">}}{{< glossary "strike water temperature">}}{{< /tooltip >}} of 160&deg;F, since a simple rule of thumb is to use a {{< tooltip "strike temperature">}}{{< glossary "strike water temperature">}}{{< /tooltip >}} that is 11&deg;F higher than your {{< tooltip "target mash temperature">}}{{< glossary "target mash temperature">}}{{< /tooltip >}}.

Once I have my 4.5 gallons of water mixed in the grains with a good stir of my big giant spoon, I set a timer for 60 minutes. 

{{< foldergallery src="img/blog/pilsner_brew_day_july2019/add_water_to_tun" >}}

I check on the temperature of my {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}} which needs to be at 170&deg;F. I’m using a well cleaned (PBW again) Webbers temperature probe since I didn’t want to invest in too much more than the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} on this brew.

When the 60 minute timer alerts me that the {{< tooltip "mash">}}{{< glossary "mash">}}{{< /tooltip >}} is done, I drain the liquid into my {{< tooltip "brew kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}} nice and slow. I only get about 3 gallons in my {{< tooltip "brew kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, so I know there will be plenty of {{< tooltip "sparging">}}{{< glossary "sparge">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to do!

The temperature of my {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}} was a little high, so I added a little bit of room temperature spring water to bring it down to 170&deg;F. At that point I try to gently pour the heated {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}} into the {{< tooltip "tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to rinse the grains and start heating more {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}}!

I’ll let the {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}} in the {{< tooltip "tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} hang out till the next batch of {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}} heats to 170&deg;F.

Somewhere in the second batch of {{< tooltip "sparge water">}}{{< glossary "sparge water">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, I achieved 5.5 gallons in my {{< tooltip "kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}}. I’ll drain the rest of the water in my {{< tooltip "tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} into the sink, and use the {{< tooltip "spent grains">}}{{< glossary "spent grains">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to make [dog biscuits]({{<ref "/guides/2-brew_dog_biscuits.md">}})! 

{{< foldergallery src="img/blog/pilsner_brew_day_july2019/filling_the_kettle" >}}

Now it’s time to bring my {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to a boil!

For this recipe, once I achieve a boil, I will be adding my {{< tooltip "bittering hops">}}{{< glossary "bittering hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, allow the {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to boil for 45 minutes, then adding the {{< tooltip "aroma hops">}}{{< glossary "aroma hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and {{< tooltip "whirlfloc tablet">}}{{< glossary "awhirlfloc tablet">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and boiling for another 15 minutes. When I add the {{< tooltip "aroma hops">}}{{< glossary "aroma hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, I will also be adding my {{< tooltip "wort chiller">}}{{< glossary "wort chiller">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to the {{< tooltip "kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to sterilize it in the last 15 minutes of the boil.

While I’m thinking ahead and am waiting for my {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to boil, I’m taking my spray bottle full of StarSan and spraying down the inside of the {{< tooltip "fermentor">}}{{< glossary "fermentor">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and all the pieces. This is a no rinse product, so I drench everything and let everything sit open and in a good draining position.

Before I add the {{< tooltip "hops">}}{{< glossary "bittering hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and start the timer, I spray down a clean plate with StarSan to use for resting my giant spoon. When I add the {{< tooltip "bittering hops">}}{{< glossary "bittering hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, I already have the spoon in the {{< tooltip "kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, anticipating the {{< tooltip "wort's">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} attempt to boil over on me. Once the {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} settles back down, I spoon the {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} along the edge of the {{< tooltip "kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}} to make sure all of the {{< tooltip "hops">}}{{< glossary "bittering hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}} is in the {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, since at the boil over attempt, some of the {{< tooltip "hops">}}{{< glossary "bittering hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}} got stuck to the side. Since I have a trusty Google home mini nearby, I call out my instructions to set a timer for 45 minutes while I do this spooning.

I grab my garden hose and run it into my kitchen to get it hooked up to the {{< tooltip "wort chiller">}}{{< glossary "wort chiller">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, since I want to have it ready to put in at the end of the 45 minute timer.

{{< foldergallery src="img/blog/pilsner_brew_day_july2019/adding_ingredients_to_boil" >}}

I run some StarSan through the hose that will hook up to the {{< tooltip "kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and drain in the {{< tooltip "fermentor">}}{{< glossary "fermentor">}}{{< /tooltip >}}. It is the same hose that I used to drain the {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} from the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}} into the {{< tooltip "brew kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, but once the {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} is made I get sanitizer heavy. Since I’m in sanitization mode, I find the airlock and sanitize it too. While I’m at it, I spray down the {{< tooltip "wort chiller">}}{{< glossary "wort chiller">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, even though I intend on it being sanitized by being in the {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} for the last 15 minutes of the boil.

When my 45 minute timer ends I add the {{< tooltip "aroma hops">}}{{< glossary "aroma hops">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, {{< tooltip "wort chiller">}}{{< glossary "wort chiller">}}{{< /tooltip >}} and {{< tooltip "whirlfloc tablet">}}{{< glossary "whirlfloc tablet">}}{{< /tooltip >}}, then start a new timer for 15 minutes. I do all this as quickly as possible (while taking pictures).

{{< foldergallery src="img/blog/pilsner_brew_day_july2019/add_wort_chiller" >}}

At the end of the 15 minute timer, I quickly remove the {{< tooltip "brew kettle">}}{{< glossary "brew kettle">}}{{< /tooltip >}} from the heat and onto a hot plate while a helper runs out to turn on the garden hose to run cold water through the {{< tooltip "wort chiller">}}{{< glossary "wort chiller">}}{{< /tooltip >}}.

First gravity reading was at 1.050, and the {{< tooltip "target gravity">}}{{< glossary "target gravity">}}{{< /tooltip >}} is 1.045 so I am going to dilute with a little bit of bottled spring water. After adding some spring water, I get an {{< tooltip "original gravity">}}{{< glossary "original gravity">}}{{< /tooltip >}} reading of 1.046 which I am satisfied with.

Sprinkle the yeast a little bit at a time and giving it a good stir between each sprinkle and the {{< tooltip "fermentor">}}{{< glossary "fermentor">}}{{< /tooltip >}} is ready for me to seal up and top with an airlock.

I place the {{< tooltip "fermentor">}}{{< glossary "fermentor">}}{{< /tooltip >}} in my basement where it has an ambient temperature of 15&deg;C. A little warmer than the recommended range in the recipe, but the yeast strain will work. The {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} is extremely cloudy and definitely has some hops floating around, so I plan on emptying out the little bulb at the bottom of the {{< tooltip "fermentor">}}{{< glossary "fermentor">}}{{< /tooltip >}} in a couple days.

{{< foldergallery src="img/blog/pilsner_brew_day_july2019/end_boil_add_yeast" >}}

Final volume in the {{< tooltip "fermentor">}}{{< glossary "fermentor">}}{{< /tooltip >}} was just under 4 gallons. I had lost a lot due to evaporation. Next time I’ll collect at least 6.5 gallons of {{< tooltip "wort">}}{{< glossary "wort">}}{{< /tooltip >}} from the {{< tooltip "mash tun">}}{{< glossary "mash tun">}}{{< /tooltip >}}!
